SalesPilot Application User Guide

Welcome to the SalesPilot user guide. This application is designed to manage products and services its sales, inventory, and analytics with a user-friendly menu layout. Use the sections below to understand each feature and menu of SalesPilot.

Product Management

To add a new product, follow these steps:

  • Enter the product details, including its name, category, unit price, and other details in the form.
  • Once a product is added, its Category and prices should be re-updated when the page refreshes, then click the update button to display the latest details.

Sales Management

To record sales transactions, follow these steps:

  • Enter the product name as its written in the products records and enter the unit price, sales quantity, and total price and other sales data.
  • Submit the sales data. This data will update in the Sales and Inventory sections, ensuring inventory levels reflect the sale.

Data Categories: Adding and Viewing

In addition to products and sales, you can manage other key data categories:

  • Products: Access the Category Menu to view products grouped by category for easier management.
  • Sales & Stock: View and manage all sales transactions and stock levels within the Inventory Menu.
  • People: Manage Customers, Staff, and Suppliers through the People Menu, where you can add or update contact and profile information as needed.
  • Expenses: Record expenses related to sales or inventory operations. This information will be used for financial analytics.

Analytical and Performance Reporting

To track key performance indicators, the following sections provide analytical insights and reporting:

  • Charts and Reports Menu: Access real-time charts and detailed reports, showing overall analytics on product performance, revenue, and profitability.
  • Performance Analysis:
    • Product-Based Sales Metrics: See product metrics for individual products, helping you identify top-performing items.
    • Inventory Metrics: View available stock, sell-through rates, and inventory turnover for each product.
    • Category-Based Sales Metrics: Analyze sales by product category to assess which categories are contributing most to overall sales.
    • Expenses: View expenditures which is the combinaton of expenses and total cost of products .
    • Invoices: Monitor product and service deliveries and purchases with the invoices and suppliers menu.
